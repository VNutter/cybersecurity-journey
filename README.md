# Cybersecurity Journey

## About Me

I’m transitioning into cybersecurity after building a career in leadership, operations, and security. I’m currently pursuing a Bachelor’s in Cybersecurity with a concentration in Data Analytics Fundamentals. This is the beginning of that journey, and I’m documenting it as I learn.

## Why Cybersecurity?

The puzzle aspect is what hooked me. CTFs feel like solving a mystery; there’s a flag hidden somewhere, and success comes from thinking creatively, asking questions, and approaching problems from different angles. I enjoy the process of understanding how systems work just as much as finding the solution.

## Current Goals

* Complete my cybersecurity coursework and Data Analytics Fundamentals concentration
* Build a strong foundation in networking, Linux, and security fundamentals
* Become comfortable with CTF platforms such as TryHackMe, Hack The Box, and picoCTF
* Connect data analytics concepts with cybersecurity through log analysis, threat detection, and visualization
* Explore different career paths to discover where my skills and interests fit best

## Learning Roadmap

* Networking fundamentals (TCP/IP, ports, protocols, routing)
* Linux command-line fundamentals
* Data Analytics Fundamentals coursework
* Beginner CTFs and hands-on labs
* Security+ and foundational security concepts
* Log analysis and SIEM fundamentals
* Home lab projects and incident investigations
* First complete CTF writeups and security projects

## Repository Contents

This repository documents the learning process rather than presenting polished work. It will include:

* Notes from coursework and self-study
* CTF writeups, including both successes and challenges
* Small scripts and projects as new skills are developed
* Home lab documentation
* Reflections on what worked, what didn’t, and what to learn next

> If I look back at this repository a year from now and realize I’ve outgrown this README, that means I’ve been learning.
